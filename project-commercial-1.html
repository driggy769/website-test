Project Overview: Scanshot Marketing Email Campaigns

ScanShot is a recurring marketing email campaign produced for SCAN, designed to promote key commercial activity and keep subscribers informed of offers, events, and partner-led initiatives. The email was sent approximately three times per week to a large subscriber base and also archived on the main SCAN website, making it a highly visible and business-critical piece of marketing collateral.

The content of ScanShot varied frequently and was designed to be flexible enough to support a wide range of use cases. These included promotional sales campaigns, announcements for industry events such as trade shows or in-house activations, and client-funded promotions where technology partners (for example, NVIDIA) would request visibility for specific product launches, such as the release of the RTX 40-series graphics cards.

My Role & Responsibilities

I worked closely with the marketing team to design, build, and deliver each ScanShot email from concept through to deployment. My role covered both visual design and front-end email development, ensuring consistency, accuracy, and reliability across all sends.

Briefing & Concept Development

Each ScanShot began with a detailed brief from the marketing team. This outlined the overall concept of the email, the products or promotions that needed to be featured, and any mandatory content such as specific wording, brand guidelines, or partner-supplied imagery.

Using this brief, I translated the requirements into a clear visual direction, ensuring the design aligned with SCAN’s brand identity while remaining flexible enough to accommodate a wide variety of content types. Particular care was taken to balance promotional impact with clarity and readability, given the fast-paced nature of the campaign and its frequent distribution.

Design & Approval Process

I created initial design mockups in Adobe Photoshop, laying out the full email structure, hierarchy, and visual treatments. These mockups were shared with the marketing team for feedback and approval, allowing for iterations where necessary to ensure the final design met both internal expectations and external partner requirements.

This stage was critical for maintaining consistency across the campaign while still allowing each ScanShot to feel timely, relevant, and tailored to its specific message.

Development & Testing

Once the design was approved, I coded the email using custom-built templates that I had developed and maintained. These templates were designed with flexibility and reusability in mind, allowing for quick turnaround times while ensuring compatibility across major email clients.

After development, I produced and sent test emails to the marketing team for review. This allowed us to check layout accuracy, content correctness, and overall presentation before final approval. Any necessary refinements were made at this stage to ensure a polished final output.

Deployment & Archiving

Following approval, the ScanShot email was deployed to the full subscriber base. Each edition was also archived on the main SCAN website, extending its lifespan beyond the initial send and providing an accessible record of past campaigns.

Outcome & Impact

ScanShot became a dependable and scalable marketing channel, capable of supporting high-frequency sends without compromising quality. The structured workflow — from briefing to design, development, testing, and deployment — ensured efficiency while maintaining a consistently high standard of execution.

This project strengthened my ability to work at pace within a marketing environment, collaborate effectively with cross-functional teams, and manage the full lifecycle of a digital marketing asset. It also reinforced the importance of adaptability, as each ScanShot required tailoring to different audiences, partners, and commercial objectives while still fitting within a cohesive brand framework.
