About Me

I’ve been creative for as long as I can remember. As soon as I could hold a pencil, I was drawing — filling sketchbooks with cartoons and dreaming of becoming an animator. That early love for visuals slowly evolved into a fascination with design, and by the time I was a teenager, my goal was clear: I wanted to be a graphic designer working on something I loved, whether that was a video game magazine like EDGE or a guitar magazine like Total Guitar.

At 16, I landed my first design internship. It was there that a senior designer gave me a piece of advice that would quietly shape my entire career: “You should have a website portfolio, not just a physical one.” I couldn’t afford to pay someone to build it, so I did what felt natural — I taught myself how to make one.

That decision changed everything. The website not only showcased my design work, but it also opened the door to my first junior web designer role. From there, my career naturally shifted toward development, and I began working across a range of industries including publishing, ecommerce, property marketing, and more.

Over the years, I’ve been fortunate to work with some incredible brands, including Santander, AVIVA, NVIDIA, AMD, Microsoft, Chivas Regal, and Harrods. Each project has pushed me to grow — not just as a developer, but as a creative problem-solver.

Today, I’m a web developer who continues to learn, experiment, and refine my craft. While my technical skills have grown with every role, I’ve never let go of what started it all — a deep passion for design and creating things that look great, feel intuitive, and work beautifully.
