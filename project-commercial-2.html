Brand Pages

Project Overview: Dynamic Brand Pages & Landing Pages

A core part of my day-to-day role involved designing and building dynamic brand pages and landing pages for SCAN. These pages played a key role in supporting marketing initiatives, partner campaigns, and event promotions, acting as high-visibility destinations for both paid and organic traffic.

The pages were often client-funded, meaning they needed to adhere closely to partner requirements while still maintaining SCAN’s brand standards. Campaigns ranged from the promotion of new hardware releases such as graphics cards and CPUs, to game launches and co-branded initiatives. In addition to commercial campaigns, landing pages were also created to support industry events that SCAN was either attending or hosting.

My Role & Responsibilities

I was responsible for the end-to-end delivery of these pages, covering design, front-end build, and deployment to staging for review. This involved working with multiple stakeholders across the business and balancing creative requirements with technical and brand constraints.

Briefing & Stakeholder Collaboration

Briefs were provided by a range of teams depending on the nature of the project. Most commonly this was the marketing team, but requests also came from the 3XS team or, on occasion, directly from senior management when pages were required for high-profile events or time-sensitive campaigns.

Each brief outlined the purpose of the page, required content, brand or partner guidelines, and any specific goals such as promoting a product launch, highlighting an event, or driving engagement around a release.

Design & Mockups

Using the provided brief, I designed page layouts and visual concepts in Adobe Photoshop, creating clear and structured mockups that demonstrated hierarchy, layout, and overall visual direction. These mockups were shared with the requesting stakeholder for review and feedback.

This stage allowed for collaborative iteration, ensuring that the final design aligned with both internal expectations and any external partner requirements before moving into development.

Development & Deployment

Once designs were signed off, I translated the approved mockups into fully functional web pages. These were coded and deployed to the staging environment, where they could be tested and reviewed prior to going live.

This process ensured accuracy, responsiveness, and consistency with existing site standards, while also allowing time for final checks and refinements before release.

Outcome & Impact

These dynamic brand and landing pages became a reliable foundation for a wide range of marketing and commercial activity. By following a structured workflow — from briefing and design through to development and staging — I was able to deliver pages efficiently while maintaining a high level of quality and flexibility.

The project strengthened my ability to work with diverse stakeholders, manage competing priorities, and deliver polished digital experiences that supported both brand and business objectives.
